**APT (Advanced Persistent Threat):** A highly organized, long-term cyberattack usually sponsored by nation-states.  
**Zero-Day:** A previously unknown vulnerability that has not been patched and can be exploited.  
**Botnet:** A network of compromised devices controlled remotely to perform attacks.  
**Ransomware:** Malware that encrypts data and demands payment for decryption.  
**Phishing:** A social engineering attack that tricks users into revealing sensitive information.  
**Insider Threat:** Security risk that originates from within the organization, such as employees or contractors.  
**Denial of Service (DoS/DDoS):** An attack that overwhelms systems or networks, making them unavailable.  
**Vulnerability:** A weakness in software, hardware, or processes that can be exploited.  
**Exploit:** A technique or code used to take advantage of a vulnerability.  
**Threat Actor:** An individual or group carrying out malicious cyber activities.
